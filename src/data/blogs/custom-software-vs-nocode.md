No-code tools are genuinely excellent. For internal tools, landing pages, and early MVPs — no-code is often the right call and we say so to clients. But we also see a consistent pattern: businesses that built on no-code at early stage hitting hard ceilings at growth stage.

### Where No-Code Breaks
The ceiling appears in three places. First, performance at scale — most no-code platforms abstract away infrastructure in ways that become expensive or slow under real load. Second, integration complexity — when your fourth or fifth requirement hits, you start building Rube Goldberg machines with Zapier that break. Third, and most critically, ownership — your business logic lives inside a third-party platform's database.

### The Real Question
The question isn't no-code versus custom. It's: at what stage does the cost of no-code constraints exceed the cost of custom development? For most scaling businesses that point arrives around Series A or when you hit your first major enterprise client who asks about your data architecture.
